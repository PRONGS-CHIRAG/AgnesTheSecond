Agnes generates actionable recommendations by analyzing your entire supply chain.

Consolidation — reduce supplier fragmentation by routing more volume through one preferred supplier.
Risk Mitigation — address single-source dependencies and supplier concentration risks.
Substitution — swap similar ingredients across companies to unlock better pricing or reduce risk.
Cost Optimization — switch to a cheaper supplier while maintaining quality and compliance standards.
?
Consolidation means multiple companies buy the same ingredient from different suppliers. Agnes finds the supplier that already serves the most companies and recommends routing everyone through them — so you can negotiate better volume pricing and simplify logistics.

Each card shows the ingredient, how many companies use it, the current supplier spread, and the recommended single supplier to consolidate on.
?
Agnes groups ingredients that could potentially replace each other, so companies can coordinate sourcing.

Variants — different forms/grades of the same core ingredient (e.g. "Vitamin C Ascorbic Acid" vs "Vitamin C Sodium Ascorbate"). These share similar names and belong to the same category. Swapping between variants is usually straightforward.

Functional — different ingredients that serve the same functional role (e.g. different types of thickeners or sweeteners). These aren't name-similar but belong to the same category and could be interchanged depending on formulation requirements.

Each group lists the interchangeable members, how many companies use them, and a confidence score based on name similarity and category match.

Agnes flags supply chain vulnerabilities that could disrupt your operations.

Single Source — an ingredient is only available from one supplier globally. If that supplier has issues, there's no fallback.
Supplier Concentration — one supplier provides a large share of ingredients to many companies. Over-reliance on them is risky.
Critical Ingredient — an ingredient is used by many companies/products, making disruptions high-impact.
Supplier Quality — a supplier has low quality or compliance scores that may affect product standards.
Price Volatility — an ingredient's price swings significantly across orders, creating budget uncertainty.
